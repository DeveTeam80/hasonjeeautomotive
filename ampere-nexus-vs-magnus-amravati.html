Ampere Nexus vs. Magnus: Which is the Best Electric Scooter for Amravati Roads?

  • Home |
  • Ampere Nexus vs. Magnus
November 3, 2025

The electric scooter revolution is in full swing in Amravati, and Ampere is leading the charge. As more people look for an eco-friendly, cost-effective way to navigate the city, two names keep popping up: the Ampere Nexus and the Ampere Magnus. But which one is right for you?

As Amravati's trusted Ampere EV partner, we at Hassonjee Automotives hear this question daily. While both are fantastic scooters, they are built for different riders. Let's break down the comparison to help you choose the perfect EV for your Amravati commute.


Meet the Contenders

Think of the Ampere Nexus and Magnus as two talented siblings. They share the same great Ampere DNA but have distinct personalities.

The Ampere Nexus: The Premium Tech Commuter

  • This is Ampere's premium offering, designed for the modern rider who wants features, range, and style. It’s built for longer commutes and makes a statement.

The Ampere Magnus: The Practical City Workhorse

  • This is the practical, reliable, and agile choice. It’s perfect for daily city errands, zipping through traffic, and offers fantastic value for money.

Ampere Nexus vs. Magnus: At a Glance

The best way to see the difference is a side-by-side comparison.

Feature Ampere Nexus Ampere Magnus
Certified Range 136 km* 121 km*
Display 7-inch TFT Touchscreen Bright LCD Digital Display
Key Features Bluetooth, Navigation, Multiple Ride Modes Lightweight, Large Seat, Powerful Motor
Top Speed 93 km/h* 50 km/h*
Best For Long-distance commuters, Tech-savvy riders City errands, Daily short commutes, Families

Deep Dive: Why Choose the Ampere Nexus for Amravati?

The Ampere Nexus is your pick if your daily life involves:

  • Longer Commutes: If you travel from one end of Amravati to the other (e.g., from Vilas Nagar to the MIDC area), the Nexus’s superior range (136 km) gives you unmatched peace of mind.
  • A Need for Speed: With a top speed of 93 km/h, the Nexus is more than comfortable on Amravati's wider roads and ring roads.
  • Love for Technology: The 7-inch TFT display is a game-changer. Getting turn-by-turn navigation directly on your scooter's dash is perfect for exploring new parts of the city without pulling out your phone.

Deep Dive: Why Choose the Ampere Magnus for Amravati?

The Ampere Magnus is the smart, practical choice if your day looks like this:

  • Zipping Through Traffic: The Magnus is lighter and incredibly agile. Navigating the busy lanes of Jaistambh Square or the market area becomes effortless.
  • Daily Errands: Dropping kids at school? Grabbing groceries? The Magnus's large, comfortable seat and ample boot space make it the perfect family and utility scooter.
  • Value for Money: The Magnus delivers an excellent range (121 km) and all the essential features you need for city riding, all at a very attractive price point.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better for long-distance in Amravati, the Ampere Nexus or Magnus?
The Ampere Nexus is the clear winner for longer commutes in Amravati. It offers a significantly longer certified range (136 km) compared to the Magnus (121 km). If your daily travel takes you across town, the Nexus provides greater peace of mind.
Is the Ampere Magnus good for busy city traffic in Amravati?
Absolutely. The Ampere Magnus is known for its lightweight build and agile handling, making it perfect for navigating busy Amravati streets like Vilas Nagar Road or Jaistambh Square. It's a practical and zippy scooter for daily city errands.
Where can I test ride the Ampere Nexus and Magnus in Amravati?
You can test ride both the Ampere Nexus and the Ampere Magnus at Hassonjee Automotives. We are an official Ampere dealer in Amravati. Visit our showroom to get a feel for both scooters and decide which one is right for you.
What's the main difference in features between the Nexus and Magnus?
The Ampere Nexus is the more premium, feature-rich scooter. It comes with a large 7-inch TFT touchscreen display with Bluetooth connectivity and navigation. The Magnus features a practical, bright LCD display. The Nexus is aimed at the tech-savvy rider, while the Magnus focuses on practicality and value.
