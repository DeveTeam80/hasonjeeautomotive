Buying an EV in Amravati? 5 Things to Check Before Visiting a Showroom

Petrol prices in Amravati have crossed ₹105/liter, and more riders from Rajapeth to Badnera are making the smart switch to Electric Vehicles (EVs). But buying an electric scooter isn't just about picking the color.

With so many new showrooms popping up in Amravati, how do you know which bike will last? At Hassonjee Automotives, we have been serving Amravati’s automotive needs for years. Here are the top 5 things you must check before booking your new EV.


1. Certified EV Service Center in Amravati (Don't Buy Without This)

Many new EV brands are sold by "fly-by-night" dealers who operate out of small shops. What happens when you need a brake repair or battery check after 6 months?

The Check: Before buying, ask the showroom: "Do you have an in-house service center with trained mechanics?"

  • At Hassonjee Automotives, we don't just sell Ampere scooters; we have a fully equipped garage on Vilas Nagar Road to service them.


2. Electric Scooter Mileage in Amravati: Real vs. Claimed Range

Don't be fooled by the "IDC Range" (Indian Driving Cycle) printed on brochures. That is tested under ideal conditions.

The Reality: Amravati roads have speed breakers, traffic at Irwin Square, and flyovers.

  • Certified Range: Might say 120km.
  • True Range: Usually 85-100km.
  • Our Advice: Always ask for the "True Range" for city driving. Top models like the Ampere Magnus EX provide excellent real-world mileage.


3. Best EV Battery Life for Amravati Summer (Warranty Check)

The battery is the heart (and the most expensive part) of your EV. In Amravati's extreme summer heat (45°C+), battery quality matters.

What to Look For:

  • Removable Battery: Can you take it out to charge in your flat? (Great for apartment owners).
  • Warranty: Ensure you get at least a 3-year warranty on the battery.
  • Safety: LFP batteries are generally safer in high temperatures than older NMC types.


4. Electric Bike Price in Amravati (Check On-Road Hidden Costs)

The "Ex-Showroom Price" is never the final price.

When you visit an EV showroom in Amravati, ask for the "On-Road Price Breakdown." Check if they are charging extra for:

  • Standard Accessories (Side stand, mirrors should be included).
  • RTO Registration charges (Low-speed bikes don't need this, but high-speed ones do).
  • Insurance premiums (Compare them online).


5. Ampere Spare Parts & After-Sales Support in Amravati

Many imported EV kits don't have spare parts available in India. If your plastic panel breaks or a motor fails, you might wait months for parts.

The Hassonjee Promise: Since we deal in Ampere (by Greaves), a trusted Indian brand, spare parts are readily available at our Amravati center.


Conclusion: Visit the Top EV Bike Showroom in Amravati

Buying an EV is a smart financial decision that saves you roughly ₹30,000 to ₹40,000 per year on fuel. But the dealer you choose is just as important as the bike.

Ready to test ride? Visit Hassonjee Automotives at Vilas Nagar Road to test drive the latest Ampere Electric Scooters today. Experience the silence, the power, and the savings!

FAQ's

How much does it cost to charge an EV bike in Amravati?
Charging an EV battery takes about 2-3 units of electricity. At Amravati's electricity rates, a full charge costs roughly ₹15 to ₹20, which gives you 80km-100km of riding.
Do I need a license for an electric scooter?
It depends. "Low-speed" EVs (max speed 25kmph) do not require a license or registration. However, high-speed models (like Ampere Magnus/Primus) require a valid driving license and RTO registration.
Can I wash my electric scooter with water?
Yes, modern EVs are IP67 rated (water-resistant). However, avoid using high-pressure washers directly on the dashboard or battery compartment. A normal bucket wash is perfectly safe.
