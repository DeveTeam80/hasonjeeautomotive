New Car? The Ideal Timing to Install Heat-Rejection Films

Just took delivery of your new car in Amravati? Great time to lock in comfort and protect the interiors. Heat-rejection films (often called IR/UV or “sun-control” films) block harsh solar heat and harmful UV without compromising visibility when you choose the right specification. Here’s a simple, timing-first guide so you get maximum benefits and zero headaches.


Day 0-1: Pre-Install Checks

  • Delivery inspection: Ensure the glass is free from transport residue, stickers, or micro-scratches.
  • Wash & dry: A basic exterior clean (no shampoo on glass edges) prevents dust under the film.
  • Decide your spec: Prefer non-metallic nano-ceramic for high IR rejection and signal friendliness.

Day 3-7: The Sweet Spot

This is the ideal window to install heat-rejection films. You start protecting your dashboard, seats, and trims from UV immediately, slash cabin heat before it becomes a habit to blast the AC, and establish a “factory-fresh” look that ages well.

  • Windshield: Choose a clear, high-VLT IR/UV film to retain clarity and cut glare.
  • Sides & rear: Match VLT to your comfort and local compliance guidelines; aim for high TSER/IRR rather than just darker shade.
  • Sensor safety: Keep ADAS/FASTag/GPS zones free as per OEM guidance.

If You’re Unsure About the Windshield…

Install on side and rear glass first, then drive a couple of evenings to test glare and night comfort. Add the windshield film within 1-2 weeks. This phased approach suits first-time users.


Post-Install Care: First 48-72 Hours

  • No window roll-downs for 24-48 hours (let adhesives cure).
  • Keep surfaces dry for 2-3 days; a light haze is normal and clears as moisture evaporates.
  • Clean gently after a week with microfiber; avoid ammonia-based cleaners.

Compliance & Visibility

Choose films that maintain high visibility and support safe night driving especially for the windshield. Prioritize IR/UV performance (TSER/IRR) over extreme darkness, and confirm visibility with a meter at install.

Frequently Asked Questions (New-Car Edition)

Can I install films on the delivery day?
Yes - if the glass is clean and inspected. Many owners prefer day 3-7 to finish PPF/ceramic and then do films, but day-0 installations are fine with proper prep.
Which is better for new cars ceramic, carbon, or dyed?
Nano-ceramic offers the best heat rejection, clarity, and longevity with no signal interference. Carbon is a good mid-range; dyed is budget-first but fades sooner.
Will films affect night visibility?
Not if you choose high-VLT options especially for the windshield. Films can actually reduce headlight glare and eye strain while keeping views clear.
Do films interfere with defoggers, sensors, GPS, or FASTag?
Properly installed non-metallic films don’t interfere. Installers keep defogger lines intact and leave cut-outs or safe zones for sensors/FASTag as needed.
How long does installation take and what’s the life?
Most cars finish in 1-3 hours. Premium nano-ceramic films typically last 5-7+ years with warranties against peeling, bubbling, and excessive fade.
