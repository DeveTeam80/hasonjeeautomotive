Beyond the Basics: Why Your ADAS-Equipped Car Demands Precision Wheel Alignment

  • Home |

What Is ADAS and How Does It Work?

ADAS uses a combination of cameras, sensors, radar, and computer systems to monitor the surrounding environment and help prevent accidents. These systems must interpret data accurately to provide real-time assistance and alerts. However, the correct functioning of ADAS depends on accurate calibration. Even a slight deviation in wheel alignment can mislead the system, leading to delayed or false alerts and in the worst cases, system failures.


Why Wheel Alignment Matters for ADAS

Traditional wheel alignment focuses on ensuring that your tires are set to the manufacturer’s specifications, improving fuel efficiency, tire life, and handling. But with ADAS, it’s not just about tire wear it’s about technology and safety. Here's how poor alignment can affect your ADAS:


1: Camera and Sensor Misalignment: Many ADAS sensors and cameras are calibrated relative to the direction your wheels point. Misaligned wheels can alter the reference point, causing misinterpretation of lanes or distances.

2: False or Inaccurate Warnings: A misaligned vehicle might trigger incorrect lane-departure warnings or interfere with adaptive cruise control, putting the driver at risk..

3: ADAS Calibration Failure: After any suspension work or alignment adjustment, the ADAS systems may need recalibration. At Hassonjee Automotives, we ensure your alignment and ADAS are fully synchronized.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1: What is ADAS and why does it need accurate wheel alignment?

A1: ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems) uses sensors, cameras, and radar to assist with safety features like lane assist, adaptive cruise control, and emergency braking. Accurate wheel alignment is essential because even slight misalignment can distort sensor data, leading to false warnings or system failure.


Q2: How does poor wheel alignment affect ADAS performance?

A2: Misaligned wheels can cause your ADAS cameras and sensors to misinterpret road data. This may trigger false alerts, reduce the accuracy of lane detection, or disrupt features like adaptive cruise control and automatic braking.


Q3: When should I get my wheel alignment checked for an ADAS-equipped car?

You should check alignment after hitting a pothole or curb, installing new tires or suspension, noticing uneven tire wear, feeling a pull while driving, or after any accident. Routine checks help maintain both safety and ADAS accuracy.


Q4: Can I skip alignment if my car feels fine?

A5: No. ADAS-equipped vehicles may still operate with hidden misalignment issues that aren’t noticeable during normal driving. These issues can silently compromise safety systems until a critical moment.
